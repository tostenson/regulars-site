02Description

Short blurb

Regulars is a co-op diner game with a supernatural mystery twist. Run the night shift solo or with friends: take orders, work the grill, and keep an eye on the booths, because not every customer is who they say they are.

Long description

You run a small diner on the night shift, and you know your regulars by name. You seat the booths, take the tickets, work the grill, and ring the register. It is the same nightly routine with the same familiar faces.

Each night brings a quiet problem: one of your customers isn't really a regular. They look the part and they order the usual, but something about them is off. Your notebook keeps track of everything you know about the people who come in, and comparing what you see against what you've written is how you tell a regular from an impostor. Spot the irregular before the shift ends.

Play solo or in online co-op for up to eight players. Split the kitchen and the floor, share your notebook, and argue over the suspects. Accuse the wrong customer and it costs you. Let the real impostor stay and it costs you more.

03Features

  1. Cook to ticketTake orders, grill, plate, and serve before patience runs out. The rush builds, and so does the rent.
  2. A page for every regularBuild a notebook of habits, allergies, and routines for every familiar face who walks in.
  3. Identify the irregularCross-reference what you see against what you've written, then decide who doesn't belong.
  4. 1-8 player online co-opSplit front-of-house and back-of-house with up to seven friends. Drop-in, drop-out lobbies.

04History

Regulars is the first commercial title from Toast Game Studio, a solo developer based in America. The project began in 2026 as a one-shift prototype about identifying impostors among the regulars at a small diner, and grew into the cooperative night-shift game it is today.

The game runs on Unity and is being developed in the open. Follow updates on the Steam community page and on Discord.
