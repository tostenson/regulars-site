02Description

Short blurb

Regulars is a cooperative diner-management game with a supernatural mystery twist. Run the night shift solo or with friends, take orders, work the grill, and watch the booths — because not every customer who walked in is who they say they are.

Long description

You know every regular by name. The diner opens at sundown and runs until something — or someone — closes it. You seat the booths, take the tickets, work the grill, and ring the register. The usual nightly rhythm with the usual familiar faces.

Tonight a rule arrived with the morning paper, and a notebook full of clues sits next to the register. One of tonight's customers isn't really a regular. They look the part, they order the usual, but something is off — and the only way to be sure is to cook, serve, and watch closely. Find the irregular before the shift ends; when you're sure, pull the lever and send them through the trapdoor under booth seven.

Built for solo play and online co-op for up to four friends. Split the kitchen and the floor, share your notebook, debate the suspects, and try not to purge the wrong customer — getting it wrong costs more than money.

03Features

  1. Cook to ticketTake orders, grill, plate, and serve before patience runs out. The rush builds; so does the rent.
  2. A page for every regularBuild a notebook of habits, allergies, and routines for every familiar face who walks in.
  3. A new rule every nightEach shift starts with a different rule on the back of the menu. Whoever breaks it isn't really a regular.
  4. Identify the irregularCross-reference what you see against what you've written. Watch the booth. Trust nobody.
  5. The trapdoorOne accusation per shift. Pull the lever to purge the impostor — or pay the price for guessing wrong.
  6. 1–4 player online co-opSplit front-of-house and back-of-house with up to three friends. Drop-in, drop-out lobbies.
